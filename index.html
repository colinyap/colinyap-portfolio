§01

An engineer who likes the whole stack

I'm a Year 1 MEng (Hons) student at the University of Nottingham Malaysia, and I've spent it building across as much of engineering as I can reach — mechanical assemblies, embedded firmware, control systems, computer vision, and digital logic. The common thread isn't a single field; it's that I like taking something from a rough idea to a thing that actually works.

That's meant a PID-controlled line-following robot with computer vision, a wireless robotic hand driven by a flex-sensor glove, a 3-axis claw machine, and a business card etched onto a PCB. Each one pulled me deeper into a different discipline — motor control, wireless comms, mechanical design, board layout.

Most recently I've been drawn to digital design, building a library of Verilog projects and working through hardware fundamentals from the logic level up. I keep everything open on GitHub and write up what I learn as I go.
